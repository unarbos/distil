# Kimi K2.6 Stage-2 Teacher Swap — Experiment Runbook

**Status:** PLANNING — no production change yet
**Owner:** SN97 validator team
**Drafted:** 2026-04-29
**Decision deadline:** when (a) pod compute is available for a multi-day run AND (b) the v30 axes (now live) have ≥ 1 week of production telemetry on Qwen3.6-35B as the teacher.

---

## TL;DR

Kimi K2.6 is the strongest open-weight reasoning teacher in the post-Qwen3.6 era (~1T total / ~32B active per the K2.5 lineage), but **bigger teacher is not automatically better for a 4-7B student**. The trl-distillation-trainer article and the broader capacity-gap literature (Mirzadeh 2019, Xu 2025, Gu 2023) explicitly warn that a 235B teacher is *not better* than a 30B teacher when the student is 4B-class — and that GPQA Diamond can DROP ~10pp with the larger teacher.

Three blockers must be resolved before we can even run the experiment:

1. **Tokenizer mismatch.** Kimi K2.6 uses a different tokenizer family than Qwen. Our entire validator pipeline (FKL, RKL, top-K overlap, IS-KL, EOPD, on-policy RKL, capability probe) is tied to a **shared 248,320-vocab Qwen tokenizer** assumption. Swapping teacher families requires either (a) cross-tokenizer distillation (Universal Logit Distillation / ALM, see synthesis §3.10) or (b) a second student vocab re-train. Neither is shipped.
2. **vLLM support.** We need to confirm vLLM serves Kimi K2.6 with `--max-logprobs 128` (our top-K cache requires it) and at the precision / KV-cache layout we expect. Kimi's MoE architecture differs from Qwen3's; the existing `_stub_missing_preprocessor_config` dance will not generalise.
3. **Capacity-gap risk.** Going from a 35B → 1T teacher might *regress* the student. We need a controlled A/B before any production swap.

This runbook lays out the prerequisite work, the experiment design, the success criteria, and the rollback plan so we don't accidentally damage the production validator while exploring the swap.

---

## 1. Why Stage 2 (and why now)

Stage 1 (Qwen3.5 → Qwen3.6, shipped 2026-04-29 in v29.7) was a **drop-in compatible swap**: same architecture family, same tokenizer, same vocabulary. Wall-time and memory profile barely changed and the validator pipeline runs unchanged.

Stage 2 (Qwen3.6 → Kimi K2.6, planned) is a **major teacher swap** that buys us:

- **Stronger reasoning teacher** — the synthesis report ([§3.6](2026-04-29-distillation-sota-synthesis.md)) lists Kimi K2.5 as one of two teachers brianmeyer benchmarked against, alongside GLM-5 744B. Kimi K2.6 is presumably a step up.
- **Better OPD top-K coverage** — a teacher with a sharper distribution at decision points means the student converges faster on `top_k_overlap` (now a 0.10-weight live axis). Larger teacher ≠ guaranteed better, but on average the marginal token at high-entropy positions is more informative.
- **Wider expert pool for the MoE pathway** — Kimi K2.6's 1T total / ~32B active shape gives ~30 experts to specialise across, vs Qwen3.6's ~8. Useful if we eventually adopt expert-routing-aware distillation.

But we also pay:

- **Capacity gap** — the dominant 2026 finding (synthesis §3.1) is that *the 235B Qwen teacher is not better for a 4B student than a 30B teacher*. Empirically, Pass@1 on AIME24 was identical, and GPQA Diamond *regressed* 10pp with the bigger teacher. We must verify Kimi K2.6 doesn't do the same.
- **Tokenizer mismatch** — see §3 below. Resolving this is the bulk of the engineering work.
- **Pod cost** — Kimi K2.6 needs significantly more VRAM than Qwen3.6 even at FP8. Multi-GPU pods.

The experiment exists to generate evidence for or against the swap, with a clear decision rule.

## 2. Pre-conditions before we run anything

### 2.1 Tokenizer compatibility decision (BLOCKING)

The current validator stack assumes:
- Teacher and student share the SAME tokenizer (vocab 248,320 in subnet-config.json).
- Top-K logprobs over the teacher's vocab map directly to student's vocab.
- KL / RKL / top-K overlap / IS-KL / forking-RKL / EOPD all index the same vocab space.

Kimi K2.6 uses **a different tokenizer**. Two paths:

**Path A: stay on the Qwen tokenizer (preferred for Stage 2).** Re-tokenize Kimi K2.6's outputs through the Qwen tokenizer when caching teacher rollouts. Loss: any text that round-trips imperfectly through the two BPE schemes will produce noise. Cost: cheap (one tokenization round-trip per prompt) but signal-degrading.

**Path B: cross-tokenizer distillation.** Implement Universal Logit Distillation (Boizard TMLR 2025) or Approximate Likelihood Matching (Minixhofer NeurIPS 2025). Synthesis §3.10. Cost: ~2 weeks of engineering. Benefit: clean, no signal loss.

**Recommendation:** stage the swap as Path A first to gather A/B evidence. Only invest in Path B if Path A shows Kimi K2.6 actually beats Qwen3.6 by enough margin to justify the engineering.

### 2.2 vLLM support (BLOCKING)

- Verify vLLM ≥ N.M.K serves Kimi K2.6 with `--max-logprobs 128` AND `--enable-prefix-caching` AND `--tensor-parallel-size 4+` (our pod runs 8×H100 typically).
- Test that the existing `_VLLM_DEAD_EVENT` fast-fail path triggers correctly under K2.6 if the engine deadlocks (we saw this on Qwen3.6 — see [v29.7 report](2026-04-29-v30-strategic-rollup.md)).
- Confirm Kimi K2.6's `config.json` doesn't need a `_stub_missing_preprocessor_config` workaround (Qwen3.6 needed one for its declared-but-unpublished vision config — Kimi's config schema may surprise us differently).

### 2.3 Pod sizing and budget

Kimi K2.6 ~1T total / ~32B active → at FP8 MoE quant, expect ~250GB weights. At BF16 (no quant), ~2TB. Pod sizing:

- **FP8 path (recommended for evaluation):** 8×H100-80GB pod ≈ 640GB total. Loads with headroom.
- **BF16 path:** 16×H100 or 8×H200 pod. Significantly higher cost.
- **Activations + KV cache:** budget another 100-200GB depending on batch size and prompt length.

Cost estimate (Lium pricing as of 2026-04-29): 8×H100 pod ≈ $30/hr; a full A/B experiment (3 days end-to-end) ≈ $2,200. 16×H100 ≈ $4,400. Acceptable for a controlled experiment but only if we have evidence it'll move the needle.

### 2.4 King state isolation

The Stage-2 experiment runs on a **separate evaluation pod**, not the production validator. The production composite stays anchored on Qwen3.6. We do this to avoid:

- Mid-experiment validator restart wiping state.
- A bad K2.6 round corrupting the king selection (king-canary-streak gate would catch it but fixing it is gentle if production is untouched).
- Mixing Kimi-teacher and Qwen-teacher KL nat values in the same composite_scores.json — the values are NOT comparable across teachers because they live in different distribution spaces.

State isolation:
- Separate `state-dir` on the experiment pod.
- Distinct `composite_scores.json` lineage tagged with `teacher_revision: kimi-k2.6-fp8`.
- Promotion to production ONLY after the decision rule (§5) passes.

## 3. Experiment design

### 3.1 Variants

| Variant | Teacher | Tokenizer path | Hypothesis |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| `qwen36_baseline` | Qwen3.6-35B-A3B | native | Production baseline (already running). |
| `kimi26_pathA` | Kimi K2.6 | Re-tokenized via Qwen | Path A from §2.1. Lossy but cheap. |
| `kimi26_pathB` | Kimi K2.6 | Native (Universal Logit Distillation) | Path B. Only if Path A is promising. |

Each variant runs on the same set of N student commitments to keep the comparison apples-to-apples.

### 3.2 Student selection

Pick **5 student commitments** spanning the leaderboard:

1. The current king (highest composite.worst).
2. The runner-up king-candidate (second-highest composite.worst).
3. A median challenger (50th-percentile composite.worst).
4. The Qwen3.5-4B reference (UID -1, as anchor).
5. A known-bad commitment (a recent DQ or low-axis outlier, as a sanity check).

Rationale: the comparison must catch capacity-gap regression (where the king's `gpqa_diamond_proxy` drops with the bigger teacher) AND any benefit on top-K overlap / EOPD (where the king's `top_k_overlap` rises). 5 students give us enough Pareto-front coverage without burning excess compute.

### 3.3 Round-by-round protocol

For each variant × each student:

1. Re-run the same `block_seed` on the experiment pod. This guarantees identical procedural bench items / KL prompts / judge prompts across variants — the only thing that changes is the teacher.
2. Capture the full per-student record from `pod_eval_vllm.py`.
3. Save under `/opt/distil/experiments/kimi26-stage2/round_{seed}/{variant}/`.

Per-round cost (rough): 5 students × ~7 min/student = ~35 min per variant per round. Three variants × 6 rounds = ~10 hours of pod time.

### 3.4 Metrics captured

Every metric the production validator computes, **plus** the new shadow axes:

- All ranking axes from the v30 composite.
- Shadow axes: `kl_is`, `forking_rkl`, `entropy_aware_kl`, `teacher_trace_plausibility`.
- `topk_mass` (teacher's per-position top-K coverage — directly comparable across teachers as it's a property of the teacher distribution).
- Per-axis pass-fractions for each procedural bench (math / code / reasoning / IFEval / AIME / MBPP / tool_use / debug / correction / multi_doc_synthesis / calibration / refactor / pragmatic / knowledge_v2).
- Held-out canary: GSM8K / HumanEval / BBH / IFEval / MMLU-Pro on the king under each teacher.

## 4. Decision rule

After 6 rounds × 5 students × 3 variants = 90 student-round records, we evaluate using a **conservative lexicographic rule**:

### Tier 1 — disqualifying gates (any one fails ⇒ STOP, do not promote)

- **King-canary regression > 5pp on any of {GSM8K, HumanEval, BBH, IFEval, MMLU-Pro}.** This is the same threshold the production `king_canary_streak` gate uses; we apply it to the experiment too. If the K2.6-teacher king regresses on the held-out canary vs the Qwen3.6-teacher king, that is the capacity-gap pathology and we revert.
- **Reference 4B model regression > 5pp on any procedural bench.** If the K2.6 teacher's reference axes are systematically lower, the bench items themselves are now distributionally mismatched (the bench questions are unchanged but the teacher signal is different).
- **`top_k_overlap` regression > 5pp** — a major teacher swap should *raise* top-K overlap, not lower it. Lower means the teacher's top-K is not aligned with the student's, and OPD is going to fail.

### Tier 2 — promotion criteria (all must pass to promote)

- **Mean `composite.worst` lift ≥ +0.03** across the 5 students. (~3pp.)
- **Mean `top_k_overlap` lift ≥ +0.02** on the king and runner-up. (Predicts OPD gains for miners.)
- **No axis regressing more than -0.03 on the king and runner-up.** Pareto-protective.
- **Subjective pass on the long-form judge axis** — Kimi K2.6's chat output style is acceptable to the rubric (i.e., not so different from Qwen3.6 that the rubric grade collapses).

### Tier 3 — pass conditions (any of these can also gate promotion)

- **`teacher_trace_plausibility` lift ≥ +0.05.** Indicates the student's distribution overlaps Kimi's emitted trajectories — direct OPD-success predictor.
- **`forking_rkl` lift ≥ +0.05** on the king and runner-up.

### Decision matrix

| Tier 1 | Tier 2 | Tier 3 | Action |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Pass | Pass | Pass | **Promote.** Update `subnet-config.json`, deploy. |
| Pass | Pass | Fail (some) | **Hold for additional evidence.** Run 6 more rounds with stochastic prompts. |
| Pass | Fail | — | **Hold.** Document the comparison. Re-evaluate with Path B if Path A was used. |
| Fail | — | — | **Revert + report.** Document the regression mode. Do not retry without addressing the failure cause. |

## 5. Implementation plan

### 5.1 Engineering sequence

1. **Tokenizer round-trip helper** (~1 day) — `eval/cross_tokenizer.py`. Encodes a Kimi K2.6 generation through Qwen3 tokenizer, computes alignment loss, surfaces a per-prompt `tokenizer_drift` telemetry signal.
2. **Teacher cache compatibility shim** (~1 day) — `pod_eval_vllm.py::_load_teacher_cache` should detect the teacher tokenizer family and apply the round-trip if needed.
3. **vLLM smoke test** (~0.5 day) — start a Kimi K2.6 server with `--max-logprobs 128`, run the existing `prepare_teacher_probe_refs_vllm` against it, verify no startup crash and that token IDs in the response are recoverable.
4. **Experiment pod orchestration** (~1 day) — a separate `scripts/experiments/run_kimi26_a_b.py` that runs all 3 variants × 5 students × 6 rounds and emits a comparison table.
5. **Decision-rule evaluator** (~0.5 day) — `scripts/experiments/evaluate_kimi26_a_b.py` that ingests the per-variant records, applies the tier matrix, and prints a recommended action.
6. **Production swap (if promoted)** (~1 day) — update `subnet-config.json`, restart validator, monitor for one full round to confirm no regression on the production canary.

Total: ~5 days of engineering before the experiment runs, then 3 days of pod compute, then 1 day of analysis = ~9 days end-to-end.

### 5.2 Rollback path

If the experiment passes Tier 2 and we promote, but production telemetry over the next 48h shows regression:

- Revert `subnet-config.json` to Qwen3.6 (one commit).
- Restart `distil-validator.service`.
- The experiment pod records are saved permanently for forensic analysis.
- Document the regression mode in a follow-up report.

The production validator's `king_canary_streak` gate is our last-line-of-defense — if Kimi K2.6 makes the king regress on held-out canary, the gate auto-dethrones. So even in the worst case, an unsupervised regression bounces back within ~3 rounds.

## 6. Open questions / risks

1. **MoE expert routing under our prompt mix.** Qwen3.6 has 64 experts, ~8 active per token. Kimi K2.6 has ~30+ experts. The "right" experts may not activate on our particular ClimbMix prompt distribution; the teacher might effectively be using only a 5B-class subset of itself. We should measure expert activation entropy in the experiment and report it.
2. **Distribution-shift on procedural items.** Our procedural bench items were calibrated to produce ~0.55-0.70 reference-model pass rates with Qwen3.5-4B. They may produce different rates when graded against Kimi K2.6's golden answers — even if the math is the same. If reference passes ≤0.40 on any axis, the calibration needs re-doing.
3. **Kimi K2.6 license + open-weight availability.** Verify the model is actually open-weight and can be served on our pods without restriction.
4. **MoE memory hot-swapping.** Even at FP8, Kimi K2.6 may force us to host the teacher across multiple GPUs in a way Qwen3.6 didn't. The teacher-load wall-time may double.

These need to be resolved during the §5.1 sequence — they don't block planning the experiment.
